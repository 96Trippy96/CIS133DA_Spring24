What are MOSFETs?

A MOSFET (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor) is a type of electronic switch used to control electricity flow in various devices. Imagine it like a gate that opens or closes to let electricity through, depending on whether it's "on" or "off." It's commonly used in computers, audio equipment, and other gadgets because it can switch electricity quickly and efficiently.
